In Introduction.

Cold War Era and the Soviet Union: - The Cold War was a prolonged geopolitical standoff between the United States and the Soviet Union after World War II. - Both superpowers engaged in ideological, political, and military rivalry without direct armed conflict. - The Soviets established communist regimes in Eastern Europe, leading to tensions with the West.

Significance of the Soviet Union's Collapse (1991): - In 1991, the Soviet Union dissolved, leading to the emergence of 15 independent countries. - Mikhail Gorbachev's reforms (glasnost and perestroika) triggered nationalist movements within Soviet republics. - The end of the Cold War era reshaped global politics, alliances, and power dynamics.
